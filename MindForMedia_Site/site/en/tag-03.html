Day 3

Day 3

When you look back at yesterday, you may have noticed how automatically some impulses show up. Maybe exactly when you sat down at your desk or when you did not know how to start. Those observations are important today because they lead directly to the topic of this day.

Today is not about using your phone less, but about looking more closely at how you use it. Some use is clear and purposeful; other use happens more in the background and drags on longer than planned. You will notice this difference consciously.

The task today is manageable. You mark a few phone moments during the day as active or passive. In addition, you do a short phone-free study phase. Not to "function," but to feel the comparison. At the end, you briefly note how it felt.

The goal today is not less use, but more understanding of your own.

Tasks for today

Task 1

Log at least 6 social media sessions today. * Example:* "08:10-08:15 Instagram."

Task 2

Mark each session as active or passive. * Example:* Active = writing a message, passive = scrolling.

Task 3

Do a completely phone-free study phase. * Example:* 45 minutes studying, phone in another room.

Bonus

Time your study phase and note it. * Example:* "45 minutes without interruption."

Fact of the day: When attention keeps switching, the brain feels tired faster even if the work itself is not hard.

Why this works

Active phone use has a clear purpose and a natural end. Passive use often stretches time without you noticing. That difference matters because passive use tends to drain attention without giving it back.

Each quick switch costs focus. A short phone-free study block gives you a direct comparison so you can feel the difference.

Side Fact: When attention keeps switching, the brain feels tired faster even if the work itself is not hard.

Quiz

Question 1: How do you recognize active use?

Active use is purposeful and ends after the task.

Question 2: What does each short switch during study cost?

Each switch interrupts focus and costs concentration.

Question 3: How does the bonus section describe passive scrolling?

Passive scrolling often serves as quick relief.

Reflection

Write your thoughts for yourself only. Nothing is saved.

Question 1: In which situations did I notice that my mind was "expecting something"?

Question 2: How does this expectation impulse feel in my body?

Question 3: Was there a moment when I did nothing - just noticed?

Question 4: What distracted me especially quickly today?
