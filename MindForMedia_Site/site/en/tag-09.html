Day 9

Day 9

When you think back to yesterday, you may have noticed that clear time windows can make decisions easier. Maybe the day felt more structured. And maybe there was still that moment in the evening when your body was tired but your mind did not really shut off. That inner sense of "I am exhausted, but something keeps running."

Those transitions are important today.

Today is about stimuli. More precisely, about what your phone and environment still offer your brain in the evening. Colors, motion, and constant visual change often have a stronger effect than you notice. Especially after a long study day, this can lead to a body that seeks rest while the mind stays active.

The task today is therefore intentionally calm. You reduce visual stimulation for a limited time and prepare the evening a bit differently. Not to sleep better or optimize anything, but to notice how less visual stimulation feels.

The goal today is not discipline, but relief.

Tasks for today

Task 1

Enable grayscale for 24-48 hours. * Example:* Turn off colors completely.

Task 2

Stay screen-free for 30 minutes in the evening. * Example:* Read or listen to music.

Task 3

Choose a calm alternative on purpose. * Example:* Stretch before sleep.

Bonus

Note your time of falling asleep. * Example:* "Asleep at 10:45 pm."

Fact of the day: Bright screen light can delay natural sleepiness.

Why this works

Visual salience pulls attention. Bright colors and motion keep the brain engaged, especially in the evening. Grayscale reduces the pull without needing willpower.

A short screen-free evening window helps your system transition from activity to rest.

Side Fact: Bright screen light can delay natural sleepiness.

Quiz

Question 1: What does visual salience mean?

Bright colors and motion pull attention.

Question 2: What do grayscale settings do according to the bonus section?

Removing color reduces attraction and urge.

Question 3: What is a short screen-free time in the evening for?

It helps the brain move from activity to rest.

Reflection

Question 4: How did that feel?
