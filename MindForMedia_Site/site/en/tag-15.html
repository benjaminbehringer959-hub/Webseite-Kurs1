Day 15

Day 15

When you think back to yesterday, you may have noticed that learning feels different when you are not alone. Maybe it was easier to start. Maybe you felt a bit more carried. And maybe you also noticed how much of your attention is not only taken by learning itself, but by everything that affects you alongside it.

Maybe this thought appeared: why does my head already feel full even though I have not really started yet?

Today looks exactly at that. Not at your behavior, but at the input that flows in every day. Messages, feeds, updates, stories - you take much of it in on the side, and yet your mind keeps working with it.

The task today is therefore calm and concrete. You sort your digital input a little. Not to know less or miss out, but to give your head more overview again.

The goal today is not abstinence, but relief through order.

Tasks for today

Task 1

Unfollow at least 8-10 accounts that only distract you. * Example:* Unfollow celebrity and meme pages you only scroll.

Task 2

Create a favorites list with three useful sources. * Example:* One learning YouTube, a uni newsfeed, and a podcast.

Task 3

Plan a fixed reading time (e.g., 20 minutes) for useful content. * Example:* 8:00-8:20 pm reading instead of scrolling.

Bonus

Read 10-15 pages in a printed book today. * Example:* A chapter in a textbook or nonfiction.

Fact of the day: Attention is limited and not endlessly renewable.

Why this works

Your attention is limited. The more inputs compete for it, the harder it is to stay calm. Curating your feed reduces unnecessary load.

A fixed time for useful content keeps you intentional and reduces endless decision-making.

Side Fact: Attention is limited and not endlessly renewable.

Quiz

Question 1: What is cognitive load?

Too many inputs strain attention.

Question 2: Why does unfollowing help according to the bonus section?

Less input means less mental pull.

Question 3: What does a fixed reading time do?

A fixed slot reduces decision fatigue.

Reflection

Write your thoughts for yourself only. Nothing is saved.

Question 1: How full did my head feel today?

Question 2: Was there less mental restlessness?

Question 3: Which contents took up my attention?

Question 4: What gave me an overview?
