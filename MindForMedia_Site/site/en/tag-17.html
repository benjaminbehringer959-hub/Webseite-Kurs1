Day 17

Day 17

When you look back to yesterday, you may have noticed that new small routines are not automatically stable. Maybe the new sequence worked once and not another time. Maybe you were tired, stressed, or already somewhere else in your head, and the old impulse arrived faster than the new one.

That is exactly the starting point for today. Changes are rarely linear. They wobble, especially on days when extra pressure appears. Exams, deadlines, long to-do lists, or simply a bad day can bring old patterns back.

The task today helps you find a calm way to handle that. Not to avoid setbacks, but to be prepared when they happen. You design a simple plan for such moments and observe how it feels not to be surprised.

The goal today is not control, but security in handling difficult situations.

Tasks for today

Task 1

Write one sentence about what you do immediately if you notice you scrolled. * Example:* "If I scroll: put phone away, breathe for 2 minutes, then continue."

Task 2

Practice this replacement once when the urge appears. * Example:* Pause, drink water, breathe 2 minutes instead of scrolling.

Task 3

Note briefly how it helped you. * Example:* "After breathing I could refocus."

Bonus

Inform your buddy briefly if you slipped. * Example:* "Scrolled briefly, doing 20 min extra focus now."

Fact of the day: Stress narrows attention and reduces flexibility.

Why this works

Under stress, the brain falls back on familiar patterns. That is normal. A relapse plan removes the feeling of failure and gives you a clear next step.

Observation without judgment reduces inner resistance and makes change easier over time.

Side Fact: Stress narrows attention and reduces flexibility.

Quiz

Question 1: What happens to behavior under stress?

Stress triggers old habits.

Question 2: What is part of a relapse plan?

A plan gives a clear next step after a slip.

Question 3: What does observation without judgment do?

Neutral observation reduces self-criticism and helps change.

Reflection

Write your thoughts for yourself only. Nothing is saved.

Question 1: Did a moment feel like a setback?

Question 2: How did I handle it internally?

Question 3: Did the plan give me security?

Question 4: What do I take with me?
