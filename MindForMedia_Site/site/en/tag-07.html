Day 7

Day 7

When you think back to yesterday, you may have noticed that even with more distance on the home screen, impulses do not simply disappear. Maybe there was that short moment while studying: you got stuck, your head felt tired, and the thought of the phone showed up. Not loud, more in the background.

Today is about exactly that moment. Not about avoiding or judging it, but about how you can meet it when it shows up. Impulses are not a system error. They belong to learning just like focus and breaks do.

The task today invites you to get to know replacement actions. Small things you can do when the impulse is there. Not to remove it, but to feel what happens when you offer it something else for a moment.

The goal today is not control, but experience.

Tasks for today

Task 1

Create a list with at least six replacement actions. * Example:* Stretch, drink water, step outside.

Task 2

Use a replacement action whenever the urge to scroll appears. * Example:* Instead of scrolling -> 10 push-ups.

Task 3

Do at least six replacement actions today. * Example:* Six intentional interruptions during the day.

Bonus

Mark the most effective replacement action. * Example:* Movement helps most.

Fact of the day: Even short physical activity can briefly stabilize attention.

Why this works

The urge to scroll often seeks quick relief, not content. Replacement actions give the impulse another direction without suppression.

Small physical actions can reset attention and give you a short buffer to choose what you really want to do.

Side Fact: Even short physical activity can briefly stabilize attention.
