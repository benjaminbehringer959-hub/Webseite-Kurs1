Day 20

Day 20

When you look back on the last days, you may notice that your phone use has changed. Maybe not dramatically, but noticeably. Some things feel calmer, other things more conscious. And maybe there are still areas that feel shaky. That is exactly what today is about.

Today is not a finish line in the sense of "done." It is a pause. A moment to look at what you tried as a whole. Not to judge, but to check what feels sustainable for you, even when daily life becomes fuller again.

The task today helps you go through your personal system once more and organize it in a manageable way for the next period. Not as a rigid rule set, but as orientation you can return to.

The goal today is not perfection, but a realistic plan that supports you in daily life.

Focus test

Short 5-10 minute test. Note the result for comparison later.

Tasks for today

Task 1

Think of two stressful days and decide how social media should work on them. * Example:* Exam day: social media after 7 pm, max 30 minutes; training day: only after training.

Task 2

Set fixed daily social media times for next week and write them down. * Example:* Mon-Fri 6:30-7:00, Sat 10:00-10:30, Sun 7:00-7:30.

Task 3

Create a simple 7-day plan with time windows and a short weekly review (10 min). * Example:* Plan daily 6:30-7:00 social, Sunday 7:30 review notes.

Bonus

Put the weekly review into your calendar. * Example:* "Sunday 7:30 - social media review (10 min)."

Fact of the day: Clear, simple plans are followed more often than complex ones.

Why this works

The final day is a systems check. You test your plan against stressful days and make it realistic. Stability comes from fitting your real life, not from strict rules.

A short weekly review keeps the system alive without turning it into a new burden.

Side Fact: Clear, simple plans are followed more often than complex ones.

Quiz

Question 1: What is the system test on day 20?

Question 2: What makes systems stable according to the bonus section?

Systems last when they fit real life.

Question 3: Why is the maintenance plan short?

Simple plans are easier to follow.
