Day 16

Day 16

When you think back to yesterday, you may have noticed that your head feels a bit calmer when fewer things pull at your attention at the same time. Maybe learning was clearer. Maybe you also noticed that some things happen automatically, without you consciously thinking about them.

That is exactly where today starts. A lot of what you do in daily life does not run through decisions, but through habits. Not because you choose them, but because your brain has learned patterns. These patterns do not change through resolutions, but through repetition and fitting frames.

The task today is therefore very manageable. You build a small, concrete sequence of cue, action, and a short reward. Nothing big, nothing new. Something that already fits in your day. You observe how it feels when a sequence starts to happen a little by itself.

The goal today is not consistency, but a first feeling for automaticity.

Tasks for today

Task 1

Choose a small routine after studying (e.g., stretching). * Example:* After each study phase, 2 minutes of stretching.

Task 2

Do the routine at least twice today (after two study phases). * Example:* After block 1 and block 2, 2 minutes each.

Task 3

Note briefly whether the routine helped you avoid scrolling. * Example:* "After stretching I did not want to grab the phone."

Bonus

Tie the routine to a fixed time. * Example:* After school, go outside for 5 minutes.

Fact of the day: The brain strengthens connections that are used regularly.

Why this works

Small routines become automatic through repetition. The brain strengthens paths that are used regularly. That is why tiny post-study rituals matter.

A routine gives you a predictable transition and reduces the pull toward scrolling.
