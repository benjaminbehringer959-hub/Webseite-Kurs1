Day 4

Day 4

When you think back to yesterday, you may have noticed that some types of phone use cost more energy than others. Maybe you reached for the phone especially when studying felt uncomfortable or when you did not know how to continue. Those moments matter today.

Today is about looking more closely at what actually makes you pick up the phone. The phone itself is not the center; the triggers behind it are. Often there are very specific situations or feelings that trigger the impulse, sometimes so quickly that you hardly notice.

The task today helps you make those triggers visible. You write down your most important triggers and think of one possible replacement action for each. Not as a strict rule, but as an idea. At the end you try one of those replacement actions once on purpose.

The goal today is not to avoid impulses, but to understand them better.

Tasks for today

Task 1

Write down the trigger before each opening. * Example:* "Stress after homework."

Task 2

Identify your three most frequent triggers. * Example:* Boredom, stress, waiting.

Task 3

Choose a replacement action for each trigger. * Example:* When bored -> stand up and move for 2 minutes.

Bonus

Avoid one trigger on purpose. * Example:* Do not take your phone to bed.

Fact of the day: In stressful moments the brain automatically searches for quick relief.

Why this works

Impulses are often triggered by specific moments or feelings, not by the phone itself. When you name your triggers, you create space between the impulse and the action.

A replacement action does not fight the impulse. It redirects it for a moment and gives your system a different path.

Side Fact: In stressful moments the brain automatically searches for quick relief.

Quiz

Question 1: What is a trigger?

A trigger is the moment or feeling that starts the impulse.

Question 2: What does FOMO describe in the bonus section?

FOMO is the fear of being left out.

Question 3: What is the purpose of a replacement action?

A replacement action creates a short pause and a different path.

Reflection

Write your thoughts for yourself only. Nothing is saved.

Question 1: When did studying feel easier than usual today?

Question 2: When did I notice my focus becoming fragile quickly?

Question 3: What role did my phone play?

Question 4: Was there a moment of clarity?
