Day 6

Day 6

When you think back on the last days, you may have noticed that many phone grabs are not planned. They happen in small in-between moments. For example, when you get stuck while studying, read a sentence twice, or feel tired inside. Without much thought, the phone is suddenly unlocked.

Today is about exactly that moment. Not about changing your behavior, but about what your phone offers as soon as you pick it up. The home screen is key. It influences which apps you see and which you open automatically.

The task today is very practical. You change your home screen so that impulsive opening is a little less automatic. Social apps move farther back or into folders, widgets disappear, study or productivity apps move forward. It is not about preventing anything, but about observing what shifts.

The goal today is not control, but awareness: to feel how much design shapes behavior.

Tasks for today

Task 1

Move social apps into a folder or to a back page. * Example:* Folder "Distractions" on page 3.

Task 2

Remove widgets and quick shortcuts. * Example:* Delete the Instagram widget.

Task 3

Place study or productivity apps visibly. * Example:* School app on the home screen.

Bonus

Take a before/after photo. * Example:* Screenshot of the new home screen.

Fact of the day: People reach for what is easiest to access without thinking.

Why this works

Your home screen is a form of choice architecture. What you see first is what you are most likely to open. Moving social apps away adds a tiny pause that changes behavior.

Visible study apps act as quiet cues. The point is not to ban, but to shift what your attention meets first.

Side Fact: People reach for what is easiest to access without thinking.

Quiz

Question 1: What is choice architecture?

It describes how the layout of choices shapes behavior.

Question 2: What is friction in this context?

A small extra step can reduce automatic tapping.

Question 3: What do visible study apps on the home screen do?

Visible study apps remind you of your intention.
