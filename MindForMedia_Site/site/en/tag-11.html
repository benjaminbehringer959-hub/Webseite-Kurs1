Day 11

Day 11

When you look back at the first week, you probably noticed that a lot did not change through "more discipline," but through small frames. Maybe there was that evening moment when your body was tired but your mind did not shut off. Those transitions show how strongly timing and environment decide.

Today is about the morning. The start of the day is often the moment that quietly decides how your head goes into the day. You might know this: you wake up, grab the phone "just for a second," and before you are really there, you are already in messages, feeds, or chats. Afterwards the start feels unclear, even if you cannot say why.

The task today is therefore deliberately simple. You leave the phone alone for the first 20-30 minutes after waking and use an offline alarm if possible. You also plan a short activity that sorts your head, for example a few minutes of stretching or a very short plan for the day. It is not about doing it right, but about noticing how a calmer start feels.

The goal today is not control, but clarity at the beginning of the day.

Tasks for today

Task 1

Avoid social media for 20-30 minutes after waking. * Example:* Set the alarm to 7:00, phone on the shelf, breakfast and a quick plan (7:00-7:20).

Task 2

Use the morning time for a short offline action. * Example:* Write 3 school tasks (math exercises, vocab, presentation).

Bonus

Place the phone at night so you do not grab it immediately. * Example:* Put the phone on the desk instead of the pillow.

Fact of the day: A structured morning often reduces small decisions that cost energy later.

Why this works

The first moments of the day set a tone. Small changes in the morning can reduce the number of small decisions later on.

A short phone-free window after waking gives your brain a clearer start and reduces the automatic pull of feeds.
