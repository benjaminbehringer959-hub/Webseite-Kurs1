Day 13

When you think back to yesterday, you may have noticed that even a little distance can change a lot. Maybe it was quieter, maybe just less scattered. And maybe there were still moments where your thoughts drifted. That is what today builds on.

Today is not about creating even more distance, but about using time in connected blocks. Many study phases break into small pieces in everyday life: start briefly, interrupt, start again. That often feels more exhausting than it has to, especially close to exams.

Deep work here does not mean anything extreme. It is not about hours of concentration, but about deliberately connected study blocks in which you switch as little as possible. The task today invites you to try one or two such blocks. Not to be maximally efficient, but to experience how learning feels when you do not have to restart constantly.

The goal today is not pushing through, but a sense of depth.

Tasks for today

Task 1

Plan fixed study blocks with clear times. * Example:* 3:30-4:00 math, 4:10-4:40 English.

Task 2

Prepare everything you need before each block. * Example:* Book, notebook, pen ready, close unnecessary tabs.

Task 3

Keep breaks phone-free (short movement outside). * Example:* 5 minutes at the window, 2-minute walk, drink water.

Bonus

Start with the hardest subject in the first block. * Example:* Physics first, easier vocab later.

Fact of the day: Each context switch consumes mental energy.

Why this works

Longer focus blocks create deeper processing. Preparation reduces friction and helps you enter the block more smoothly.

Every context switch drains a bit of mental energy. Fewer switches mean more usable attention for learning.
