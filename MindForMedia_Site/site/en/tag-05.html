Day 5

Day 5

When you look back at yesterday, you may have noticed that certain triggers are especially reliable. Maybe exactly when you were stuck on a task or felt pressure. Today you start there, not with yourself, but with your environment.

The focus today is notifications. Not because they are "bad," but because they often pull attention automatically, before you can even decide where you want to focus. You might know this: you read a paragraph, the phone buzzes, and even if you do not check it, the thought "I will look in a second" is already there.

The task today is very concrete and deliberately limited. You turn off notifications for social apps and disable previews. Then you observe how that feels in daily life. It is not about being reachable or unreachable, but about noticing what changes when your phone becomes a little quieter.

The goal today is not control, but experience.

Tasks for today

Task 1

Disable all social media notifications. * Example:* No push messages from TikTok or Instagram.

Task 2

Turn off notification previews. * Example:* No message previews on the lock screen.

Task 3

Write a short observation in the evening. * Example:* "I was interrupted less while studying."

Bonus

Time-limit messaging apps. * Example:* Open WhatsApp only at fixed times.

Fact of the day: Constant alertness feels mentally draining even when little happens externally.

Why this works

Notifications keep your system in a state of readiness. Even when little happens, constant alerts drain mental energy. Turning off previews adds small friction and helps your brain settle.

The goal is not to be unreachable. It is to notice what changes when your phone gets quieter.

Side Fact: Constant alertness feels mentally draining even when little happens externally.

Quiz

Question 1: What do notifications often decide for you?

Notifications decide where your attention goes.

Question 2: What does friction mean here?

Friction is a small pause that slows automatic behavior.

Question 3: What does silence make visible?

When it is quiet, you notice impulses more clearly.

Reflection

Write your thoughts for yourself only. Nothing is saved.

Question 1: How strong was my stress level today?

Question 2: What felt different during distraction compared to calmer days?

Question 3: Was there a moment when I noticed stress consciously?

Question 4: How did my body react?
