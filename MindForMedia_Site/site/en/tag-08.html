Day 8

Day 8

When you think back to yesterday, you may have noticed that impulses also show up when you have alternatives. Maybe there was a moment while studying where your head got a bit tired and the thought of the phone appeared. Not urgent, more like a quiet "right now would feel nice."

That is normal. Impulses do not need a big reason. They often arise right in transitions, between two tasks or between focus and a break.

Today is therefore not about single moments, but about time. More precisely, it is about not spreading social use across the whole day, but giving it a fixed place. Not as restriction, but as relief. When the "when" is clear, your head has fewer decisions to make.

The task today is intentionally manageable. You set a daily time window for social apps and set app limits. Then you observe how it feels. It is not about "holding" that window perfectly, but about noticing what changes when decisions are made ahead of time.

The goal today is not abstinence, but clarity.

Tasks for today

Task 1

Set a fixed social media time window. * Example:* 6:00-6:30 pm.

Task 2

Turn on app limits. * Example:* Instagram max 30 minutes.

Task 3

Use a timer or the system feature. * Example:* Screen time on the phone.

Bonus

Test a second short window. * Example:* 10 minutes in the evening.

Fact of the day: Bundling decisions in time often makes daily life feel calmer.

Why this works

Clear time windows reduce constant decision-making. When the "when" is decided in advance, your mind can stop negotiating in every moment.

App limits are not punishments. They are reminders of your own decision when temptation shows up.
